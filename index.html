About Me

I am a final-year Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, advised by Prof. Derek Hoiem.

My current research interests mainly lie in computer vision, especially in:

  • Closed-loop Planning: Complex tasks demand sequential decision-making under partial observability, where no single observation reveals the full state, and every decision must build on the history of what came before.

  • Memory Representation: How should an agent encode its past into a compact state, one that helps the present without drowning it, and can be updated if needed?

  • Experience Retrieval: How do we recall exactly the right piece of the past at minimum cost?

I'm always open to academic discussions and new collaborations. If my research resonates with you, or if you just want to connect, feel free to send me an email!
